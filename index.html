Investment Analysis

Four distinct frameworks — master all of them to invest with true conviction.

What is Fundamental Analysis?

Fundamental analysis evaluates a company's intrinsic value by examining its financial health, competitive position, management quality, and the macroeconomic environment. The core question: Is this business worth more or less than what the market is pricing it at?

Preferred by legendary investors like Warren Buffett, Charlie Munger, and Peter Lynch — investors who think in decades, not days.

BEST SUITED FOR

Long-term investors (buy & hold), value investors, retirement planners, and anyone who believes patient capital compounds over time.

📋 The 3 Core Financial Statements

Income StatementRevenue, costs, and profits over a period
Balance SheetAssets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity
Cash Flow StatementActual cash generated and spent by the business

🔍 Qualitative Factors to Evaluate

Management QualityTrack record, integrity, vision, and execution
Competitive MoatBrand, patents, switching costs, network effects
Industry PositionMarket share, barriers to entry, disruption risk
Growth CatalystsNew products, markets, or regulatory tailwinds
🏗️

Top-Down Approach

Start with the global economy → identify strong sectors → drill into specific companies. Think macro first, then filter down to the best names within winning industries.

🔬

Bottom-Up Approach

Start with individual companies regardless of macro conditions. Focus purely on business quality, management execution, and whether the price offers a margin of safety.

📐

DCF Valuation Model

Discounted Cash Flow estimates intrinsic value by projecting future cash flows over 5–10 years and discounting them to today's value using a required rate of return.

📚 Buffett's 4 Core Investment Principles

1️⃣Invest only in businesses you fully understand — your "circle of competence"
2️⃣Buy wonderful companies at fair prices, not fair companies at wonderful prices
3️⃣Look for durable competitive advantages — moats that protect the business for decades
4️⃣Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful

What is Technical Analysis?

Technical analysis uses price charts, volume data, and mathematical indicators to identify patterns and forecast future price movements. It works on three core assumptions: the market discounts everything, prices move in trends, and history repeats itself.

Used extensively by day traders, swing traders, and institutional desks for precise timing of entries and exits across all liquid markets.

BEST SUITED FOR

Active traders seeking entry/exit timing. Works best in liquid, volatile markets. Short-to-medium time horizons from hours to months.

📡 Trend-Following Indicators

SMA / EMASmooth price data to identify trend direction
MACDMomentum crossover signal for trend changes
ADXAverage Directional Index — measures trend strength
SupertrendATR-based dynamic buy/sell signal overlay

⚡ Momentum & Oscillators

RSIOverbought (>70) / Oversold (<30) signals
StochasticPrice position within a recent high-low range
Bollinger BandsVolatility envelope — squeeze signals breakout
Volume ProfileIdentifies high-activity price levels (POC)
📉

Chart Patterns

Reversal: Head & Shoulders, Double Top/Bottom signal trend exhaustion.
Continuation: Flags, Pennants, and Triangles confirm breakouts in the existing direction.

🕯️

Candlestick Patterns

Japanese candlesticks reveal real-time buyer-seller battles. Key patterns: Doji = indecision, Hammer = potential reversal, Bullish Engulfing = strong momentum shift upward.

🎯

Support & Resistance

Price levels where buyers cluster (support) or sellers cluster (resistance). A decisive break above resistance or below support typically triggers a strong, sustained move in that direction.

📖 The 3 Core Assumptions of Technical Analysis

🔮
Market Discounts Everything

All known information — earnings, news, events — is already reflected in the current price instantly.

📊
Prices Move in Trends

A price tends to continue in its direction until a significant force causes reversal. Always trade with the trend.

🔄
History Repeats Itself

Human psychology creates recurring patterns. Fear and greed are constant — so the same chart formations repeat.

9 Financial Ratios Every Investor Must Know

These ratios are the language of fundamental analysis. Master them and you can evaluate any company's financial health at a glance — just like institutional fund managers.

Valuation Ratios — Is the stock cheap or expensive?

📊

P/E Ratio

Price ÷ Earnings Per Share

The most-watched ratio in markets. Tells you how many dollars investors pay for every $1 of profit. High P/E = expensive or high-growth expectations. Low P/E = cheap or low-growth company.

✅ <20 = fairly valued · >30 = premium / growth stock
📚

P/B Ratio

Price ÷ Book Value Per Share

Compares market price to the company's net asset value on its books. Beloved by value investors — a P/B below 1.0 means you're buying assets for less than their stated worth on paper.

✅ <1.0 = potentially undervalued · 1–3 = fairly valued
📉

PEG Ratio

P/E Ratio ÷ EPS Growth Rate (%)

Adjusts P/E for the company's growth rate, giving a more complete valuation picture. A PEG of 1.0 is considered fair value. Below 1 may be undervalued for its growth; above 2 is expensive.

✅ <1.0 = undervalued for its growth · >2.0 = overvalued

Profitability Ratios — How efficiently does it earn?

💰

Return on Equity (ROE)

Net Income ÷ Shareholders' Equity

Measures how much profit the company generates from money shareholders have invested. Consistently high ROE over 5+ years signals a strong competitive moat and skilled management team.

✅ >15% = strong · >25% = exceptional compounder
🏭

Gross Profit Margin

(Revenue − COGS) ÷ Revenue × 100

Measures profitability before operating costs. High gross margins indicate pricing power — the ability to charge significantly more than it costs to deliver the product or service.

✅ Software: >70% · Manufacturing: >30% · Retail: >20%
💧

Free Cash Flow (FCF)

Operating Cash Flow − Capital Expenditure

The most honest financial metric — actual cash generated after running and maintaining the business. Unlike reported earnings, FCF cannot be manipulated by accounting choices or one-time items.

✅ Consistently positive and growing YoY — gold standard

Solvency & Health Ratios — Can it survive tough times?

⚖️

Debt-to-Equity

Total Debt ÷ Shareholders' Equity

Measures how much debt funds the business relative to equity. High D/E amplifies both gains AND losses. Always compare within the same sector — capital-intensive industries naturally carry more debt.

✅ <1.0 = conservative · >2.0 = high leverage, watch closely
🛡️

Current Ratio

Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities

Short-term liquidity test — can the company pay all obligations due in the next 12 months? A ratio below 1.0 means current liabilities exceed current assets — a serious red flag for investors.

✅ >1.5 = healthy · <1.0 = potential liquidity crisis
📈

EPS Growth (YoY)

(Current EPS − Prior EPS) ÷ Prior EPS

Earnings Per Share growth is the most closely tracked metric by institutional investors. Consistent double-digit EPS growth sustained over 5+ years is the hallmark of a great compounding business.

✅ >10% YoY = strong · >20% YoY = exceptional

Fundamental vs Technical — Complete Side-by-Side

Both methods have distinct strengths, weaknesses, and ideal use cases. The most profitable investors know when to apply each framework — and how to combine them for maximum edge.

Dimension📊 Fundamental Analysis📈 Technical Analysis
Core QuestionWhat is this company WORTH intrinsically?Where will this PRICE move next?
Time HorizonLong-term (months to decades)Short-to-medium term (hours to months)
Primary Data SourceFinancial statements, earnings, management, macroPrice charts, volume, patterns, indicators
Underlying BeliefPrice eventually reflects true intrinsic valueAll information is priced in; history repeats
Key Tools UsedP/E, P/B, ROE, FCF, DCF models, earnings callsRSI, MACD, moving averages, candlesticks, S&R
Entry DecisionBuy when price < intrinsic value (margin of safety)Buy when chart patterns confirm uptrend/breakout
Best Market ConditionStable, rational markets with clear earnings trendsVolatile, liquid markets with clear chart patterns
Primary RiskValue trap — cheap for years without recoveryFalse signals — patterns fail in low-volume markets
Famous PractitionersWarren Buffett, Peter Lynch, Benjamin GrahamJesse Livermore, Paul Tudor Jones, Stanley Druckenmiller
Learning CurveHigh — requires accounting & economics knowledgeMedium — requires chart reading & strict discipline
Works Best ForStock picking, long-term wealth building, IPOsTiming entries/exits, ETF trading, crypto & forex

✅ Fundamental Strengths

Identifies high-quality businesses before the market recognises them. Builds genuine long-term wealth through compounding. Less susceptible to market noise, manipulation, and short-term volatility. Backed by decades of academic research and the greatest investment track records in history.

✅ Technical Strengths

Provides precise entry and exit timing signals that save significant money. Works across ALL asset classes — stocks, crypto, forex, commodities equally well. Faster feedback loops enable quicker skill development. Profits both long (rising) and short (falling) markets.

🏆 The SmartInvest Hybrid Approach

The world's most consistent performers combine both disciplines. Use fundamental analysis to build a watchlist of quality companies worth owning. Then use technical analysis to time your entry precisely — buying great businesses at technically strong price points for maximum advantage.
